# HeatRelay demo video script

**Target runtime:** 2:40. Record with clear spoken audio and keep the final
public YouTube video strictly under 3:00. Use only synthetic demo descriptions.
Do not show keys, environment files, provider logs, private text, account or
billing screens, or identifying browser data.

## Shot list and spoken script

### 0:00–0:15 — Title and problem

**Shot:** HeatRelay landing page at mobile width, English, Standard mode.

**Spoken:** “Heat alerts are broad, but a person in a hot room often needs a
small set of clear next steps. HeatRelay is a multilingual Barcelona demo that
turns a short description into a safety-bounded action plan.”

### 0:15–0:35 — Compact mobile form

**Shot:** Show the three scenarios, one language selector, compact form notice,
and closed privacy/demo disclosure. Do not submit yet.

**Spoken:** “The interface keeps the first screen compact. It explains that
OpenAI processes the text, asks users not to enter identifying details, and
uses a fixed Barcelona demo point. The app does not use browser geolocation.”

### 0:35–1:05 — Russian normal plan

**Shot:** Select Russian, submit a synthetic non-urgent description, then show
the focused result heading, weather summary, and three action phases. Keep
secondary disclosures closed at first.

**Spoken:** “The same control changes the interface and the next plan language.
For this Russian normal result, HeatRelay extracts a structured profile,
combines it with backend-owned weather and policy facts, and hydrates three
phases from its immutable Russian catalog. The model does not invent public
names, addresses, URLs, schedules, coordinates, or weather values.”

### 1:05–1:30 — Standalone Barcelona place lookup

**Shot:** Choose the place scenario; emphasize that selection makes no request.
Click the explicit search once and show compact candidate cards and the closed
verification disclosure.

**Spoken:** “Place search is separate from personal planning. One explicit
request uses the fixed demo point and shows factual Barcelona candidates,
straight-line distance, verified schedule and accessibility facts, and official
links. It is not a route, ETA, recommendation, or guarantee that a place is
open or reachable.”

### 1:30–1:50 — Fixed urgent branch

**Shot:** Switch to Traditional Chinese and submit a synthetic description that
explicitly reports chest pain. Show the one complete alert and fixed `112`
contact; do not linger on external links.

**Spoken:** “A closed set of explicitly reported warning symptoms takes a
deterministic urgent path. It shows the fixed localized `112` response and
bypasses normal planning, weather, places, travel, and the second model call.
HeatRelay is informational, not medical or emergency care.”

### 1:50–2:10 — Language, RTL, and visual modes

**Shot:** Briefly switch to Arabic or Hebrew and show RTL direction, then show
Enhanced Visibility and High Contrast without submitting again.

**Spoken:** “HeatRelay bundles 25 interface and output catalogs, including four
right-to-left languages, plus Standard, Enhanced Visibility, and High Contrast
modes. Native controls, semantic structure, focus management, and response-
owned language direction are shared across the same interface.”

### 2:10–2:40 — Architecture, Codex, and close

**Shot:** Show a simple repository view or the architecture section in this
repository, then return to the deployed page and display the GitHub and
production URLs as on-screen text.

**Spoken:** “Codex supported the milestone-based implementation, strict tests,
multilingual and RTL work, production safeguards, and evidence documentation,
while I set and reviewed the product and safety boundaries. GPT-5.6 performs
two narrow Structured Output tasks for normal plans: structured extraction and
selection from backend-approved codes. FastAPI revalidates and hydrates every
public response. The source is public on GitHub, and the bounded demo is live
at heatrelay-gr1gorii.fly.dev. The 24 non-English translations are AI-assisted
drafts, not native-speaker or medical approval.”

## Final recording checks

- Duration is at least the intended 2:30 and no more than 2:45; never 3:00.
- Spoken audio is clear throughout.
- The public YouTube visibility and URL are confirmed after upload.
- No key, environment value, log, private description, response ID, account,
  payment, or billing information appears in video or audio.
- The description names both Codex and GPT-5.6 and shows a functioning project.
- Only licensed or original visuals/audio are used.
